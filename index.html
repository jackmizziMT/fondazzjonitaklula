Who We Are

A cultural foundation
rooted in Gozo

Fondazzjoni ta' Klula is a voluntary organisation based in Santa Lucija, Gozo. We safeguard the village's heritage, bring the community together through cultural programming, and share what makes this part of the island worth knowing — with everyone who's curious enough to look.

About the Fondazzjoni
Recognition

European Destination
of Excellence

Santa Luċija, Gozo was awarded the European Destination of Excellence (EDEN) by the European Commission in 2008 — one of only eight destinations ever selected across Malta and Gozo. The award recognised the village's outstanding commitment to sustainable tourism and its rich local intangible heritage. This distinction was awarded through the work of the Fondazzjoni.

Community Initiative

The Ta' Klula
Free Library

Housed within the public telephone booth in Santa Lucija's main square, next to St. Lucija Church, is a small community library managed by the Fondazzjoni ta' Klula.

The collection includes books in various languages and across a wide range of subjects, suitable for both adults and children.

We invite you to take a book to enjoy at your leisure and kindly return it once you have finished, so others may benefit. You are also welcome to contribute to the collection by leaving a book for fellow readers.

Open to everyone — take a book, leave a book

What We Do

Culture, community
and heritage

Throughout the year, the Fondazzjoni brings together residents and visitors around the Wine Festival, quarterly literary evenings with Inizjamed, seasonal heritage and nature walks, and the candlelit Luminary Evening on the feast of Santa Luċija. A programme that is growing.

01

Culture

Celebrating Gozitan culture through events, arts, and shared experiences that bring people together.

02

Heritage

Preserving and promoting the history, traditions, and natural environment of Santa Lucija and Gozo.

03

Community

Building connections between residents, visitors, and all those who care about this place and its future.
